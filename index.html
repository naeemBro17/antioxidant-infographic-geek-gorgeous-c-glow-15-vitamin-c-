Mechanism of Action — The Antioxidant Network
Vitamin C (L-Ascorbic Acid) — hydrophilic; neutralizes reactive oxygen species (ROS) in the aqueous cell compartment
Vitamin E (Alpha-Tocopherol) — lipophilic; protects cell membranes and lipid bilayers from UV-induced oxidative damage
Ferulic Acid — proton donor; rapidly sacrifices electrons to neutralize free radicals, regenerating and stabilizing both C & E — extending shelf life by months
This photoprotection factor represents biological ROS defense — not UV ray absorption or blockage.
Not a sunscreen substitute. Designed to neutralize oxidative stress that passes through SPF filters.
